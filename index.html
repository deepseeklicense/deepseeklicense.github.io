DeepSeek Lisence FAQ

中文版 / English Version

Since their release, the DeepSeek series of open-source models have attracted widespread attention from the tech community, with many developers conducting derivative development based on the DeepSeek model or building upper-layer applications. Meanwhile, we have also received inquiries about our model open-source licenses and usage restrictions. To help everyone further understand our open-source policy and alleviate concerns about using the DeepSeek models, this article will answer some of the most common questions.

Introduction to DeepSeek Open Source License

DeepSeek applies different licenses for code repository and model:

  • Code: MIT license
  • Model: A custom open-source license（Example）

The MIT license applied to the code is a standard license and will not be elaborated further here. The custom license used for the model is modified based on the OpenRAIL license, characterized by its adaptability, openness, and responsibility:：

  • Adaptability, focusing on model parameters and weights, rather than code.
  • Openness, with permissive open-source conditions and restrictions, encourages the sharing, utilization, and distribution of open-source achievements, stimulating more creativity.
  • Responsibility, advocating for the adoption of behavior-based usage restrictions in the license to reduce the risks of harm from artificial intelligence technology.

Open Source Policy FAQ

Here are some common questions and answers for developers using this license.

Can DeepSeek open-source models be used for commercial purposes?

YES.

All current DeepSeek open-source models can be used for any lawful purpose, including but not limited to direct deployment, derivative development (such as fine-tuning, quantization, distillation) for deployment, developing proprietary products based on the model and derivative models to provide services, or integrating into a model platform for distribution or providing remote access.

Will DeepSeek charge fees or claim a share of the profits from users of the open-source models?

NO.

DeepSeek open-source models are completely free, with no licensing fees. Any rights to derivative models, product applications, and model-generated materials developed based on DeepSeek open-source models belong to the user, and DeepSeek will not claim any profits or benefits generated by the user's use.

Do I need to apply or register with DeepSeek before using the open-source models?

NO.

Developers can freely obtain and use DeepSeek open-source models without any application or registration with DeepSeek.

Do I need to give a notice when distributing derivative models or products based on DeepSeek open-source models?

YES.

The DeepSeek license requires notice conditions. It is recommended that users provide a copy of the license in their distributed derivative models or released products through notice, product terms, or other appropriate means.

Do I need to open source the derivative model developed based on DeepSeek open-source models?

NO.

The DeepSeek license differs from "copyleft" licenses such as the GPL in that it does not require the open sourcing of derivative models, allowing users the freedom to choose whether to open source or not.

After modifying or developing the DeepSeek open-source model, is it required to distribute the derivative model under the same DeepSeek license?

NO.

If the derivative model or product continues to be distributed in the open-source community, developers are free to choose the applicable license.

What are the restrictions on using DeepSeek open-source models?

The DeepSeek license, consistent with current mainstream open-source model licenses, requires avoidance of illegal or dangerous purposes, as detailed in the license's Attachment A (Use Restrictions).
